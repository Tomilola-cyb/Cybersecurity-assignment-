Recent Cybersecurity breaches
1. Yale New Haven health system data breach
On March 8, 2025, Yale New Haven health system identified a massive data breach affecting 5.6 million patients. An unauthorized third party accessed IT systems and stole sensitive data, including Social Security numbers, demographic details, and medical record numbers and demographic data. The incident resulted in a proposed $18 million settlement.
What happened? 
On March 8, 2025, YNHHS detected "unusual activity" within its information technology (IT) systems.
What was the cause of the breach? 
An unauthorized third party gained access to the network and, on that same day, exfiltrated files containing patient data.
What was the impact? 
The breach exposed a variety of Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and Protected Health Information (PHI): 
* Demographics: Full names, dates of birth, physical and email addresses, and phone numbers.
* Identifiers: Social Security numbers (SSNs) and Medical Record Numbers (MRNs).
* Other Data: Race, ethnicity, and patient type.
* Exclusions: No financial accounts, payment information, or employee HR data were accessed
What was the outcome? 
While patient care remained unaffected, the breach led to an $18 million class-action settlement finalized in March 2026.

2. Marks & Spencer (M&S) Ransomware Attack
In April 2025, Marks & Spencer (M&S) suffered a massive ransomware attack that paralyzed its digital operations and supply chain for weeks. The breach, attributed to the "Scattered Spider" group, resulted in a staggering £300 million loss in operating profit. While the company restored services by August 2025, it faced significant reputational damage and legal challenges from affected customers.
What happened?
Over the 2025 Easter weekend (April 19–21), M&S identified a "highly sophisticated" ransomware attack that encrypted critical servers and forced the company to take its online ordering systems, mobile app, and Click & Collect services offline.
What was the cause of the breach?
The attack was executed through a social engineering campaign where hackers impersonated an employee to trick a third-party IT contractor into resetting credentials. This allowed the "Scattered Spider" group to deploy DragonForce ransomware across the company’s VMware ESXi infrastructure.
What was the impact?
The breach caused massive operational chaos and the exposure of personal data:
* Operations: Online shopping and the M&S app were suspended for seven weeks; contactless payments and supply chain logistics were severely disrupted.
* Customer PII: Names, dates of birth, physical and email addresses, phone numbers, and order histories were compromised.
* Staff Data: Internal names and email addresses were accessed.
* Exclusions: No passwords or usable payment card details were stolen.
What was the outcome?
The incident resulted in a £300 million hit to operating profits due to lost sales and recovery costs. M&S collaborated with the Metropolitan Police and the NCSC to restore systems, eventually resuming full digital services in August 2025. The company later faced multiple class-action lawsuits regarding the protection of customer data.
